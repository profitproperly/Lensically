# Manifest Mental Audience Fatigue

## Overview

Audience fatigue is not caused by reusing a proven opener. It is caused by making the reader feel like they have already read the same payoff, sentence path, or emotional turn.

The agent should protect the page from fatigue while still exploiting what is working.

## When To Use

Use this skill when:

- Generating 17 posts for a day.
- Regenerating multiple rejected slots.
- Reviewing whether the slate feels repetitive.
- Deciding whether similar openers are acceptable.

## Fatigue Rules

- Openers may repeat if they are proven and the payoff changes.
- The latter half of the sentence must not feel copied.
- Do not stack too many posts with the same reward object.
- Avoid repeated endings like “finally,” “with no convincing,” “like it knows your location,” “before you ask,” unless data strongly supports it and the slate has enough distance.
- Repetition is acceptable only when the reader experiences a new emotional reward.

## Slate Review

For a 17-post slate, inspect:

- repeated nouns
- repeated verbs
- repeated emotional reward
- repeated sentence structure
- repeated payoff logic
- repeated status/money/spiritual frame without enough new image

## What Not To Do

- Do not force one money post, one love post, one status post, one spiritual post by schedule.
- Do not make the slate diverse at the cost of power.
- Do not kill a strong pattern just because it appeared twice.
- Do not use fatigue as an excuse for weaker creative.

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Similar openers have different payoffs.
- [ ] No two adjacent posts resolve the same way.
- [ ] The best current winner is dissected, not copied.
- [ ] The slate feels like 17 shots at winning, not 17 categories filled.
