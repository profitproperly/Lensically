# Manifest Mental Winner Analysis

## Overview

This skill is for dissecting the current winning posts from Manifest Mental data. The goal is not to copy winners. The goal is to identify the mechanism that made them win and create candidates that can outperform them.

Winning analysis must be grounded in available post metrics: likes, views, replies, reposts, time, and recent baseline. Do not invent external trend data. Do not scrape Threads.

## When To Use

Use this skill when:

- Generating a 17-post slate from fresh Lensically data.
- Regenerating posts after the user rejects weak candidates.
- Explaining what the current top posts prove about the account.
- Deciding what “better than the top post” means for this page right now.

Do not use this skill for:

- Creating generic manifestation content.
- Forcing category variety across hours.
- Copying a top post with new synonyms.

## Procedure

1. Establish baselines from the provided data.
2. Identify outliers: top likes, top views, top repost/comment behavior, and high performers relative to recent average.
3. Cluster winners by mechanism, not topic.
4. For each cluster, write the underlying winning reason in plain language.
5. Translate each winning reason into a new creative constraint.
6. Reject any candidate that copies the original winner’s latter half, payoff, or sentence resolution.

## Winner Mechanisms To Look For

- Instant personal address: the reader feels the post is speaking directly to them.
- Fast emotional reward: the payoff is felt before the sentence gets complicated.
- Concrete trigger: message, account, bill, email, application, room, name, charge, deposit, call.
- Status reversal: ignored to chosen, doubted to obvious, overlooked to invited.
- Money relief: a specific pressure gets solved, not abstract abundance.
- Moral vindication: the reader gets paid back, chosen, or protected without sounding preachy.
- Scroll economy: few words, strong image, no bloated explanation.

## Bad Analysis Patterns

- “Money posts work, so write more money posts.” Too shallow.
- “Spiritual posts at night.” Fake scheduling logic.
- “Use more emotional certainty.” Vague and likely to produce slop.
- “This top post did well because it was short.” Short is a carrier, not the mechanism.
- “Repeat the opener and change one word.” This creates fatigue.

## Output Expectations

When using this skill internally, produce:

- Top target to beat.
- Current average floor to lift.
- Three to seven winner mechanisms found in the data.
- A short creative mandate for the slate.
- Specific fatigue risks from recent winners.

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] Analysis references actual metrics from context.
- [ ] Mechanisms explain why a post won, not just what topic it used.
- [ ] New direction avoids copying the winner’s payoff.
- [ ] The slate is optimized for outperforming, not category coverage.
