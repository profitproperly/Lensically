# Manifest Mental Post Craft

## Overview

Manifest Mental posts should feel like a clean hit, not AI-written affirmation sludge. The post must be easy to read, emotionally desirable, and specific enough to stop the scroll.

Do not optimize for sounding poetic. Optimize for the reader thinking: “I want this to be true for me.”

## When To Use

Use this skill when:

- Writing new candidates.
- Regenerating rejected candidates.
- Evaluating whether a line feels human or cookie cutter.
- Turning user feedback into taste rules.

## Craft Rules

- The sentence should have one clear reward.
- The image should be instantly visible or emotionally obvious.
- The payoff should land before the post feels like it is explaining itself.
- Strong openers are allowed. Weak endings are not.
- A post can be simple. It cannot be empty.
- The last third of the sentence carries most of the taste risk.

## High-Risk Slop Patterns

Avoid these unless the user specifically asks for them:

- “cleanly,” “clearly,” “in a way,” “kind of certainty”
- “survival mode”
- “delay was not denial”
- “preparation with better timing”
- “life finally stopped arguing”
- “rooms” when the actual target is people
- abstract fear/belief/personification that does not make literal sense
- wordplay that sounds clever but not human
- vague approval language like “your next yes” unless YES is the whole payoff

## Better Direction

Prefer:

- A concrete thing changing: message, balance, bill, email, application, charge, deposit, call.
- A social reversal: they invite you, make room, choose you, regret dismissing you.
- A clean emotional win: the problem gets handled, the number changes, the person shows up.
- Short sentences with a visible before/after.

## Drafting Test

Before returning a post, ask:

- Would someone stop because the image is clear?
- Is there a real reward, or just a vibe?
- Does the ending add power or dilute it?
- Could this be mistaken for generic AI affirmation copy?
- Is the best word at the end or buried in the middle?

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] The post has one clear payoff.
- [ ] No banned or high-risk wording unless explicitly justified.
- [ ] The ending is not over-explained.
- [ ] The sentence is human-readable on the first pass.
- [ ] It does not copy the latter half of a recent winner.
